%d or %i: Signed decimal integer (e.g., int, short, long).
%ld to accept input of long integers
%lld to accept input of long long integers
%u: Unsigned decimal integer.
%o: Octal integer.
%x or %X: Hexadecimal integer (lowercase or uppercase letters for digits a-f).
%f: Floating-point number (e.g., float, double).
%e or %E: Floating-point number in scientific notation (lowercase or uppercase 'e').
%g or %G: General format for floating-point numbers (uses %f or %e depending on the value).
%c: Single character.
%s: String (null-terminated character array).
%p: Pointer address.
%%: Prints a literal percent sign.
